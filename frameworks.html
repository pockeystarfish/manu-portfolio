01

Friction-Based
Strategy Model

Traditional strategy frameworks assume clean execution. The Friction-Based Strategy Model starts from the opposite premise: every system has embedded friction, and identifying these friction points is the first step to unlocking performance.

Instead of starting with goals and working backward, this framework maps the complete operational system first, identifies where friction exists (delays, bottlenecks, information asymmetries, misaligned incentives), and designs targeted interventions to remove or reduce that friction before scaling.

The Process

01

Map the System

Document every node, flow, and dependency in the operational system. No assumptions.

02

Identify Friction Points

Locate delays, bottlenecks, information asymmetries, and misaligned incentives.

03

Quantify Impact

Measure the cost of each friction point in time, money, or opportunity lost.

04

Intervene & Measure

Deploy targeted solutions, measure results, iterate.

02

Execution-to-Outcome
Pipeline

Why do brilliant analyses die in slide decks? The gap between insight and outcome isn't an intelligence problem — it's an engineering problem. This framework bridges the execution gap with a systematic pipeline.

Every strategic insight passes through four stages: Translation (converting analysis into actionable specs), Architecture (building the operational infrastructure), Deployment (launching with feedback loops), and Measurement (tracking against commercial KPIs). Each stage has explicit handoff criteria.

Pipeline Stages

01

Translation

Convert strategic analysis into operational specifications with clear deliverables and owners.

02

Architecture

Build the operational infrastructure — workflows, tooling, data pipelines, team structure.

03

Deployment

Launch with embedded feedback loops. Fast iteration, tight measurement, rapid course correction.

04

Measurement

Track against commercial KPIs. Revenue, retention, cost reduction, or whatever the strategy targeted.

03

Green Portfolio
Diversification Logic

ESG-compliant investing shouldn't mean accepting lower returns. This framework demonstrates how AI-driven portfolio construction can satisfy sustainability mandates while optimizing for risk-adjusted performance.

By cross-referencing ESG ratings, real-time market data, and sector correlation matrices, the model identifies portfolio compositions that maximize Sharpe ratios within sustainability constraints — proving that green and profitable aren't mutually exclusive.

Core Logic

01

Constraint Definition

Set ESG minimum thresholds, sector exposure limits, and risk tolerance parameters.

02

Multi-Signal Analysis

Cross-reference ESG scores with financial fundamentals, momentum signals, and correlation data.

03

Optimization Engine

Run constrained optimization to find the portfolio maximizing risk-adjusted return within green bounds.

04

Dynamic Rebalancing

Continuous monitoring and automated rebalancing as market conditions and ESG ratings shift.

04

AI Workflow
Optimization Framework

Most AI implementations fail not because the models are bad, but because the workflows around them are poorly designed. This framework ensures AI tools are embedded into operational systems, not bolted on top.

The framework maps existing workflows, identifies where AI adds genuine value (not just novelty), designs integration points with clear human-AI handoffs, and measures impact against pre-AI baselines. The goal: AI that makes the system faster, not more complex.

Implementation

01

Workflow Audit

Map the current process. Time each step. Identify where human effort is highest-cost, lowest-value.

02

Value-Add Analysis

For each step, assess whether AI genuinely improves speed, accuracy, or cost — or just adds complexity.

03

Integration Design

Design AI touchpoints with explicit input/output contracts and human review gates.

04

Baseline Comparison

Deploy AI-augmented workflow. Compare performance against pre-AI baseline. Iterate or rollback.
